Introduction

English grammar has evolved from a number of sources such as German, Spanish and even French. Modern English grammar is relatively simple. However, a few points may present a challenge for learners, namely tenses. English has a number of tenses and passive voice companions thereof which do not exist in other languages at all or if they do, they manifest themselves in different grammatical forms. Some tenses are becoming increasingly less common in spoken English.

We recommend studying through the material in order presented here. This approach guarantees a step-by-step, structural and most comprehensive aquisition of the material. However, should one feel a certain level of familiarity with a particular topic, there is no point in staying with that topic for too long and one may move on to the subjects which require more careful attention.

To Be Present

The verb Be (am, is, are) can be both a meaning (main) verb and an auxiliary verb:
He is at school right now. Main verb meaning location.
I am a teacher. Main verb meaning working as/having an occupation.
They are eating tacos. Auxiliary verb in Present Continuous tense.

The form of the verb Be changes as follows:
Personal Pronoun Positive (+) Form of To Be
I am
he is
she is
it is
we are
you are
they are

  • Action is in process now
  • Action is happenning not right now but around now
